About the Event

PlaceIT, our flagship ideathon, is a two half-day event that offers a platform for students to develop and pitch innovative solutions to real-world problems. The event features expert-led sessions on ideation and project building, followed by a pitching round where participants showcase their ideas. A thought-provoking talk show with industry professionals further enriches the event, providing valuable insights into emerging technologies and creative thinking.

Expected Reach

  • 60 — 80 active participants
  • Students from diverse engineering disciplines
  • IEEE and graVITas social media exposure
  • Direct engagement with a technology-driven student community

Guidelines

Team Formation & Eligibility

Team Size: Teams must consist of 2 to 4 members. Solo participation is not permitted to encourage collaboration.

Eligibility: Open to all undergraduate and postgraduate students across disciplines. Cross-specialization teams are highly encouraged.

Submission & Code of Conduct

Original Work: All ideas and design frameworks must be conceived during the event. Plagiarism leads to disqualification.

Deadlines: All submissions must be uploaded to the official portal before Day 2 morning deadline.

Professionalism: Maintain professional decorum during sessions and interactions.

Pitching Rules

Time Limits: Teams will have exactly 7 minutes to pitch, followed by a 2-minute Q&A session with the judges. Strictly enforced.

Presenters: At least two members of the team must speak during the pitch presentation.

Structure & Timeline

Day 1 — Ideation, Hacking & Pitch Prep

09:00 AM — 10:00 AM

Launch & Keynote

Event kickoff, opening remarks, and the official release of the problem statements.

10:00 AM — 11:30 AM

Masterclass: Ideation to MVP

Expert-led session on design thinking, scoping down features, and building a project framework.

11:30 AM — 12:30 PM

Hacking Kickoff & Team Sync

Teams form, brainstorm, and begin their initial build.

12:30 PM — 02:00 PM

Lunch Break

02:00 PM — 04:00 PM

Deep Dive Hacking & Mentorship

Core development block. Mentors circulate for active checkpoints and technical troubleshooting.

04:00 PM — 05:00 PM

Pitch Prep & Day 1 Wrap-up

Teams pivot from coding to storytelling, drafting their slide decks for the following morning.

Day 2 — Refinement, Pitches & Grand Finale

09:00 AM — 10:00 AM

Final Sprint & Submission

One final hour to squash bugs, polish slides, and officially submit project links/decks.

10:00 AM — 11:30 AM

Round 1: Preliminary Pitches

Parallel judging tracks operate simultaneously. Each team gets a strict 4-minute pitch + 2-minute Q&A.

11:30 AM — 12:00 PM

Deliberation & Networking Break

Judges tally scores to select finalists. Finalists are announced at 12:00 PM.

12:00 PM — 01:30 PM

Grand Finale: Top Finalists Pitch

The chosen finalists take the main stage to pitch directly to the entire main jury panel.

01:30 PM — 02:00 PM

Closing Ceremony & Awards

Final remarks, judge feedback, and the announcement of the winners.

Evaluation Criteria

Pitches will be scored out of 50 points based on the following rubric:

  • Innovation 10 Points
  • Feasibility 10 Points
  • Impact & Scale 10 Points
  • MVP / Design 10 Points
  • Presentation 10 Points
  • Total 50 Points
